Maui Fire Research

Join us in creating a comprehensive understanding of the Maui wildfires to help make Maui safer.

About the Project:
As a key member of the University of Hawaii Maui College (UHMC) team, I am deeply involved in the Maui Fire Research Initiative. This project, crucial for understanding and mitigating the wildfire challenges in Maui, especially after the Lahaina fires of August 2023, is underpinned by the support from the National Science Foundation through Rapid Response Research (RAPID) grants. Our work is at the intersection of advanced technology and local wisdom, aiming to devise strategies that enhance community safety and wildfire preparedness.

My Contribution:
Working closely with my data science professor, three classmates, under the leadership of Alice Koniges and David Eder, and collaborating with other prestigious institutions, my role encompasses fire modeling, comprehensive data collection, engaging with the Maui community, and thorough analysis of gathered information. This collaborative effort is dedicated to building a detailed model of wildfire spread and its interaction with urban and natural landscapes.

Collaborative Efforts:
Our team at UHMC is not working in isolation. We're part of a larger network that includes experts from institutions such as the UH Manoa, University at Buffalo, University of Nevada Reno, and the National Center for Atmospheric Research (NCAR), among others. This collaboration allows us to leverage a wide range of expertise, ensuring our research is robust, comprehensive, and actionable.

Oceanit: Climate Change Toolkit at Paia Mantokuji

As a student ambassador / intern at Oceanit, I engage in vital climate action using our Climate Change Toolkit at the historic Mantokuji in Paia. This suite of strategies and software solutions, including ArcGIS, Python, and MATLAB, empowers us to mitigate shoreline erosion and adapt to environmental changes. Our work, deeply rooted in science and community collaboration, aims to preserve Mantokuji's natural beauty and cultural significance for future generations.
